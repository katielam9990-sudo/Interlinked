## Research reading list — digestible, annotatable

All picks are short reads or watchable videos, not paywalled journal slogs. Skim, annotate on the iPad, bank the good lines. Grouped so you can see which Interlinked angle each one feeds.

### The constellation metaphor (why "connect scattered thoughts" is real neuroscience)

This is your strongest *hook* material — the science literally describes what your app does.

- **Video — "The Aha! moment" / insight neuroscience explainer** *(5-min read version)*: [The Neuroscience of Aha! Moments](https://www.biosourcesoftware.com/post/5-min-science-the-neuroscience-of-aha-moments). The right anterior superior temporal gyrus fires when the brain connects *distantly related* information — the exact move Interlinked prompts.
- **Read — the accessible research summary**: [Creativity and the default network (PMC)](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC4410786/). The default mode network is active during rest and mind-wandering and helps combine memories and concepts without conscious effort. In plain terms: the unfocused, reflective state your app creates is the same brain state linked to insight.
- **One-liner you can annotate and quote:** *"Insight isn't sudden — it's the culmination of brain states running at different time scales."* Perfect voiceover line over a constellation forming.

### Why you're building it this way (autonomy, not addiction)

Feeds the "isn't competing with TikTok on TikTok's terms" angle — good for a slightly spicier, opinion-forward video.

- **Video — RSA Animate, "Drive"** (10 min, genuinely fun to watch): [Drive: the surprising truth about what motivates us](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=u6XAPnuFjJc). Autonomy, mastery, purpose — the three intrinsic motivators. This is why Interlinked leans on user-driven prompts and a non-competitive constellation instead of streaks and leaderboards.
- **Read — the 4-minute companion**: [Autonomy, Mastery, Purpose (The Marginalian)](https://www.themarginalian.org/2013/05/09/daniel-pink-drive-rsa-motivation/). Easy to annotate, quotable.

### Expressive writing (the most credible "this actually works" claim)

Decades of studies. Use this when you want authority, not novelty.

- **Video — APA "Speaking of Psychology" with James Pennebaker**: [Expressive writing can help your mental health](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=SsTzXB8M8fg) (also as an [article](https://www.apa.org/news/podcasts/speaking-of-psychology/expressive-writing)). Writing about thoughts and emotions produces measurable psychological *and* physical health benefits — 15–30 min a day. The nuance you already flagged: *how the prompt is framed* changes whether it helps, which is a direct argument for your prompt-writing being the soul of the product.

**A caveat to keep in your back pocket** (you already know this, but it matters on camera): all of this says the *category* is evidence-based, not that Interlinked itself is clinically validated. On TikTok, "built on the science of X" is fair game; "clinically proven to Y" is not. Keep the claim on the research, not the app.

## TikTok hook — research edition

*A separate lane from Video 1 (your founder story). This is the "smart, curious BME student shares a cool brain fact and ties it to what she's building" register — the drip's content pillar. Post within 2 days.*

**Format:** talking-head over a screen recording of a constellation forming, or you annotating on the iPad.

**Hook options (first 2 seconds — pick one):**

1. *"There's a specific brain network that lights up when you connect two unrelated ideas — and I accidentally built an app around it."*
2. *"Your best ideas don't come when you're focused. Here's the neuroscience of why — and what I did with it."*
3. *"I'm a biomedical engineering student, and this is the actual brain science behind the app I'm building."*

**Rough script (~30–40 sec):**

> Hook (option 1 or 2).
>
> "It's called the default mode network. It's most active when you're *not* focused — resting, walking, letting your mind wander. And it's the network linked to insight: the brain literally connecting distant, unrelated information into something new. [beat] That 'aha' feeling? It's not random. It's the culmination of brain states running underneath the surface.
>
> I've been building an app called Interlinked around exactly this. You get one prompt a day, turn your scattered thoughts into a single point on a constellation map — and over time you start seeing connections you didn't know were there. It's basically giving that brain network a place to work.
>
> I'll link the research below. Still early, still building it myself — but this is the science I keep coming back to."

**On-screen text ideas:** "the default mode network" · "active when you're NOT focused" · "the neuroscience of the aha moment" · "→ Interlinked"

**Caption:** short, curious, link the research + your app. Watch **saves and comments over views** — per your own brief, small-but-right beats big-but-wrong.
